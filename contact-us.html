Redesigning the "Contact Us" process

Background

We published a Contact Us form in 2018 and quickly became one of our most used forms with an average of 700 submissions a month.

The form could be found at the top right-hand corner of the website's header. Once a user completed the form, it would send an email to our Customer Services email inbox. They would then have to manually store that email within a document management system before being distributed to the team by management. A Customer Services advisor would respond to them one by one. This process was intended as a quick fix that would be iterated over time. However, due to other projects and resources available, this never happened.

This project looked at the end-to-end journey from a user finding the form on the website through to our Customer Services team being able to respond to the user.

This project aimed to create a frontend pattern that can be accessed on any page on the website which will route a user to a relevant web page or to a contact us form to enable a user to contact the relevant department.

Team make up

The project team had an Interaction Designer, a Junior Designer, and two Developers.

My Role

I had a few roles in the project. I was the Interaction Designer and one of the Developers.

I also led the delivery of this project. I ran this project using Agile methods by creating a backlog, splitting work into two weekly sprints, and allocating work to members of the project team. At the end of each sprint, I hosted a retro to understand any blockers and achievements so that we could improve for the following sprint.

I mentored and coached the Junior Designer throughout this project. They expressed that they wanted to learn more about other design disciplines as they were not sure which discipline suited them the most. I suggested that they worked as a Content Designer in this project so that they can experience how they work with an Interaction Designer and Developers. This gave them the chance to learn content best practices in a real-world and practical environment. I set up weekly one-to-ones with them to check in on their progress, create goals, help them tackle any challenges whilst making sure they weren't overloaded by their workload.

Interaction designer

I was responsible for mapping out user journeys, creating initial concepts, making low fidelity wireframes and prototypes through to high fidelity prototypes, iterating them based on research and feedback.

Developer

After the design phase, I became the Developer building the prototypes for the website. I used HTML, SASS, and Javascript to create the website frontend.

There was a second developer that focused on the form and backend integrations.

The problem

The form

The form itself was a major problem. Our Customer Services team had daily phone calls from the public who were struggling to contact a department digitally or by phone because of the way the form was built.

The first page had a dropdown list for a user to select what department they needed. A series of buttons would then appear which directed users back to the website, despite their being explanatory text and "Cancel" and "Next" buttons underneath. This created a loop between the website and form which frustrated users - ultimately blocking them from contacting us.

Admin time

A lot of the Customer Services team management team's time was taken up by the current process. It took anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour per day to store and distribute a user's email to an advisor.

This would usually mean that a user submitting the form would have to wait an average of two working days for a response.

Transaction requests

We also found that it was being used for transactional requests such as reporting missed bins or reporting fly-tipping despite there being online forms to do this. Just over 50% of the Contact Us forms completed were for transactional requests.

Website content

There was no content on the website advising a user how to contact us. There was a link to the form in the header, however, users often missed this on content pages.

My solutions

I worked with the team to create a concept that would tackle these issues. I made a high level process map to outline how the front end of the process would work and look like.

Admin time

I wanted to avoid a user telling us what department they’re trying to contact within the form when they’ve already selected a department from the web page. I found a way to pass a value from a URL from the new webpage into the new form which populated a hidden field in the background. The other developer in the team used this value and integrated the form submission into the document management system. This approach removed the admin time from the customer services management team.

Transaction requests and website content

Another issue to tackle in this project involved reducing the amount of transactional requests. I prototyped a concept that would route users to parts of the site to complete a transaction - reporting a missed bin for example - only leaving an option to contact us if there was no transaction request. I came with a design where users would select a department from a menu. A second menu of items relating to the department would appear next to it (on desktop). Once a second menu item was selected, content would appear that would either give them some information, direct them to a transaction or direct them through the contact form. This approach also added content to help inform the user on what they needed to do.

The three column design on desktop was not mobile friendly as it made the text far too small to read or understand. I came up with the design below where the three headings stack on top of each other and each column would replace the column before it until the content was revealed.

I also changed how a user would get to the process. Before, it lived in the top right hand corner of the header as well as having a redirect page that led to the contact form within the customer services section. I removed the option from the header and left the option in the footer. I also replaced the redirect page in the customer services section. I moved the customer services section (also known as find and contact us) from below the fold to a higher position closer to the fold as I still wanted users to be able to contact us.

This project is in live beta and can be found here.

What happens next

The next phase of this project will look at whether there are any usability issues that weren’t found during testing. I’ll use Hotjar and form submission data to inform any design decisions.
